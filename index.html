Places to Climb near St. louis, MO

Missouri

These will be the areas inside Missouri which are nearby for rock climbing

Elephant Rock

Elephant's rock is located about an hour and half away from St. Louis. The rocks are all granite, leading to a unique climbing experience in Missouri. It is primarily bouldering.Roped climbing is prohibited from the park. There are several V1 climbs as well as a high concentration of interemediate to advanced climbs. It is commonly known for high ball climbs as well.

Here a couple pictures of climbs in Elephant Rock

Robinson Bluffs

Robinson Bluff is located in cadet, MO, just south of st. Louis. It is fairly new for climbing. The climbs are said to be developed for climbers by climbers. Robinson Bluff requires a permit and a helmet to climb. There is very little bouldering. So top rope or lead climbing gear is usually required.

Johnson Shut‑ins

Johnson Shut‑ins is located in Reynolds County missouri. Its a state park that happens to have some great climbing. It is considered to be top rope only climbing due to permitting. Howver there are some amazing routes capable of being lead climbed. It has everything from easy beginner routes to the expert climber all using the same rope usually. It does offer great opens for trad leads as well but no sport climbing.

Illinois

Illionis, specifically southern Illionis is home to some amazing climbing. Being covered by the Shawnee National Forest has allowed for tons of natural boulders and rock to form allowing for a wide array of climbing.

Jackson Falls

Jackson Falls is located inside the Shawnee National. In order to get to climbing you will have to repel or go through the dog run as the bluffs are beneath the entrance level. It has some amazing routes and formations. The creeks can flow during the summer. Jackson Falls does have camping.

Holy Boulders

Holy Boulders was discovered in 2004 by Aaron Brouwer by viewing ariel photographs. the property was owned by the Tripp family. The Tripp family gave climbers verbal permission to climb. 8 years later the family decided they wanted to sell the land. in 2012 ICA started working on trying perserve the land and take ownership. The property was transferred in 2016 and is now open to climbers and has some of the best bouldering in the midwest.

Lake Kincaid

Lake kincaid is a lake in southern Illinois. There Happens to be a very big cliff coming out of the lake that provides the oppurtunity to Deep Water Solo. That is climbing with no protection due to being over the a deep body of water. you either have to hike or take a boat for Kinkaid climbing. The climbs are not beginner friendly. 5.10b is the easiet climb. Most are 5.12 or higher. You will need a floatie and towels. My favorite climbing spot near st louis.
